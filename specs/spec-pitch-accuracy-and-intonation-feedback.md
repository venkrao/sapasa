## Purpose

The feature gives a singer the same real-time and post-phrase feedback a teacher provides when they say *"you were flat on that note"* or *"your intonation drifted in the middle of that phrase"* — without interrupting the flow of singing.

***

## What It Observes

The system listens continuously while the singer is performing an exercise or phrase. It tracks:

- **Whether the sung pitch matches the target pitch** at any given moment
- **How far off** the pitch is, and in which direction (sharp or flat)
- **Whether the pitch is stable** or wavering in an unintended way
- **How the pitch moves over time** — does it arrive cleanly, drift mid-note, or settle late?
- **Where in the phrase** the problems consistently occur

***

## What the Singer Experiences

### During Singing
Feedback during the performance should be minimal and non-distracting. The singer should not be reading a screen while singing. A small, peripheral indicator — something that can be noticed in the corner of vision without breaking focus — is enough. Think of it like a tuner needle: you glance, absorb, look away.

The indicator communicates three states only: in tune, slightly off, significantly off. No numbers, no labels while singing.

### After the Phrase
Once the phrase ends, the full picture opens up. The singer sees a visual replay of what their pitch did compared to what it should have done. This is the equivalent of a teacher saying *"let me show you what happened there."*

This replay should feel like a story, not a data dump. It highlights the most significant moment — the biggest drift, the most consistent flatness — and names it plainly in plain language. Something like: *"You tend to go flat at the top of your range on this phrase"* or *"The second note arrived sharp and then corrected — try placing it more gently."*

### Over Multiple Sessions
Patterns that appear once are not worth surfacing. Patterns that repeat across sessions are the teacher's most valuable insight. The app should quietly accumulate these and, after enough repetitions, say something like: *"You've been consistently flat on F4 across the last four sessions. This might be worth focusing on."*

This is the equivalent of a teacher's memory — the thing a self-directed singer using a simple tuner app completely loses.

***

## Tone and Language

All feedback is observational, not judgmental. The app never says "wrong" or "bad." It describes what happened: direction (sharp/flat), magnitude (slightly, significantly), and location (which note, which moment in the phrase). Praise is specific too — *"that phrase was clean throughout"* is more useful than *"great job."*

***

## Boundaries of This Feature

The system only addresses pitch. It does not try to infer *why* the pitch was off — whether it was breath support, jaw tension, or something else. Those causes belong to other features. This feature is purely the ear of the teacher: it hears what happened and reports it faithfully.
